Kodeus is built around one idea: build once, earn forever. This page explains the economic model.

## The model

Every agent, tool, and logic module on Kodeus is an on-chain asset with attribution baked in. When someone uses your asset, you earn. When someone forks it and someone else uses the fork, you still earn.

## Revenue streams for creators

- **Direct execution.** Per-run or subscription fees for your agent.
- **Tool invocation.** Per-call fees for MCP tools you publish.
- **Logic module reuse.** Per-use fees for reusable logic blocks.
- **Fork royalties.** A share of downstream revenue every time your work powers a fork.

## Forking

Forking is explicitly encouraged. When a developer forks your agent:

1. The fork keeps a DNAChain link back to your original
2. Any revenue on the fork is split between you and the fork author
3. If someone forks the fork, the chain extends and upstream revenue keeps flowing

Splits default to a standard Kodeus split but are fully configurable on publish.

## Worked example

You publish a Portfolio Rebalancer.

- DAO A deploys it. You earn the full fee.
- Developer B forks it, adds a Dune dashboard. DAO B deploys the fork. Revenue splits between you and B.
- Developer C forks B's version, adds a new metric. A third DAO uses C's version. Revenue splits between you, B, and C.
- Years later, with 50 deployments across 12 forks, every original contributor is still earning.

## Anti-gaming

Rewards are tied to verified executions inside the TEE. Fake invocations cost real gas and compute, so spam is economically infeasible.

## Transparency

Every payout is logged to DNAChain. Your earnings dashboard updates in real time. Disputes are settled by reading the ledger.
